Detailed Location Types

üè¨ Freestanding Sites

Not connected to other retailers, though may be near shopping centers or other freestanding stores. Popular for warehouse clubs, hypermarkets, and fast-food with drive-through.

Advantages:
  • Greater visibility
  • Lower rents
  • Ample parking
  • No direct competition
  • Fewer restrictions
Disadvantages:
  • Lack of synergy
  • Must be destination
  • Need special offering

üèôÔ∏è Central Business District

Traditional downtown financial and business area. Many CBDs experiencing revival through gentrification.

Advantages:
  • High pedestrian traffic
  • Public transportation access
  • Business activity flow
  • Gentrification opportunities
Disadvantages:
  • Limited parking
  • Potential shoplifting
  • Slow evening/weekend traffic

üè™ Strip Centers

Attached rows of open-air stores with onsite parking. Includes convenience, neighborhood, and community shopping centers. Supermarkets typically serve as anchors.

Advantages:
  • Convenient locations
  • Easy parking
  • Lower rents
Disadvantages:
  • No weather protection
  • Less assortment
  • Limited entertainment

üí™ Power Centers

Dominated by several large anchors including full-line discount stores, off-price stores, warehouse clubs, and category specialists. Often includes freestanding anchors.

Advantages:
  • Low occupancy costs
  • High traffic
  • Large trade areas
  • Category depth
Disadvantages:
  • Intense competition
  • Limited synergy

üõçÔ∏è Enclosed Shopping Malls

Climate-controlled malls with department stores as traditional anchors. Large trade areas with diverse merchandise and entertainment options.

Advantages:
  • Weather protection
  • Large trade area
  • Diverse assortment
  • Entertainment options
  • Uniform operations
Disadvantages:
  • Higher occupancy costs
  • Management restrictions
  • Intense competition
  • Less convenient parking
  • Aging facilities

üå≥ Lifestyle Centers

Open-air configuration of specialty stores, entertainment, and restaurants with design ambience. Resembles main streets in small towns.

Advantages:
  • Easy parking
  • Appealing ambience
  • Higher-end experience
  • Entertainment value
Disadvantages:
  • Less retail space than malls
  • Smaller trade areas
  • Fewer customers

üè∑Ô∏è Outlet Centers

Contain mostly manufacturers' and retailers' outlets. Well-designed with landscaping and food courts. Tourism is an important traffic generator.

Advantages:
  • Brand names at discount
  • Tourist destination
  • Interstate access
  • Large selection
Disadvantages:
  • Remote locations
  • Limited new centers
  • Tourism dependent

üèòÔ∏è Mixed-Use Developments

Combine several uses in one complex: retail, office, hotels, residential. Pedestrian-oriented facilitating live-work-play environment.

Advantages:
  • Multiple customer bases
  • 24/7 activity
  • Pedestrian traffic
  • Community feel
Disadvantages:
  • Complex management
  • Higher development costs

üé™ Nontraditional Locations

Pop-up stores, store-within-a-store, and kiosks offer flexible, temporary, or alternative retail spaces.

Advantages:
  • Low investment
  • Short-term leases
  • Test markets
  • Seasonal flexibility
  • High traffic areas
Disadvantages:
  • Limited space
  • Temporary nature
  • Limited services

üéØ Key Takeaway

Choosing a location involves evaluating trade-offs between cost versus value to customers. Location decisions must align with the retailer's target market, shopping behavior, and retail strategy to create sustainable competitive advantage.

4

5

Think about your own shopping behavior. Identify a recent convenience shopping trip, comparison shopping trip, and specialty shopping trip you made. How did the location of each retailer influence your decision to shop there? Would you have traveled further for any of these purchases if necessary? Why or why not?
